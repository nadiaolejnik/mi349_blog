The Challenges of Life

Moving Across the World

Nadia Olejnik
Published September 9th, 2022

After living in Germany for 10 years, my parents informed my siblings and I that we are moving to the United States for my dad's job. I had just finished 5th grade and I was devastated by this news. Not to mention, I couldn't speak any English and I wasn't ready to lose all my friends and start from scratch. When my first day of school arrived, I was terrified. The entire day, I didn't understand anything people said to me. I used hand gestures the entire day to communicate with teachers and other students. However, while the first couple weeks were difficult, I soon recognized the numerous opportunities I had been provided with by moving to the United States. Not only did I form countless new friendships, but I was also fortunate to live in a country where every dream could be turned into a reality. There are many aspects of living in the United States that are not even a possibility in Germany. Therefore, even though moving thousands of miles away from the only home I had ever known, I would never exchange this experience for anything. A lot of times, people take opportunistic situations for granted, not realizing the benefits they could gain from the encounter. The main lesson I took away from this move is that it is critical that us humans learn to enter every experience with an open mind.

The Struggle of Eating Healthy

Nadia Olejnik
Published September 9th, 2022

As someone who did not grow up in the United States and was raised by the type of parents that strictly limit their children's consumption of unhealthy foods, I can confidently say that eating healthy in the United States is difficult. Not only are most menu items filled with overwhelming amounts of fat and sodium, but there are also many seemingly healthy options that are not all that healthy because they are often fried or otherwise decreased in their nutritious value. Furthermore, at most American households, there is an extreme lack of healthy meals and snacks. Whenever I visit a friend's house, I make sure to eat before because I know I will only be offered foods like pizza and chips. While it is completely fine to consume such foods every once a while, they should not consume one's main diet. However, the lack of effort and education about improving the United States' health only enhances and normalizes this lifestyle. Moreover, now that I am in college and go to the dining hall for all three meals, I have noticed the extreme lack of nutritious options. While they have a salad bar and fruit station at every dining hall, there is rarely a good selection of vegetables. When there are vegetables, they are often plain and not seasoned well, further preventing students from consuming them. Overall, I wish Americans invested more time and effort into providing tasty healthy and snacks. If there were more nutritious food options, it would be much easier for people to follow a more balanced diet.
